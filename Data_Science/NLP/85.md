# **Set 67: AI for Cyber-Physical Systems, Edge Intelligence & IoT Autonomy (Research-Level Questions)**

---

### **Q1. What defines a cyber-physical system (CPS), and why is AI crucial for it?**
**A1.**  
A cyber-physical system integrates **computation**, **control**, and **physical processes**. Examples: smart grids, autonomous vehicles, industrial automation, medical devices.  
AI enables CPS to:  
- Predict system states  
- Optimize control policies  
- Detect anomalies  
- Adapt to environmental changes  
CPS requires tight integration of AI with real-time constraints.

---

### **Q2. What is edge intelligence, and why is it needed?**
**A2.**  
Edge intelligence executes AI models directly on edge devices (sensors, microcontrollers, gateways) instead of the cloud.  
Benefits:  
- Low latency  
- Privacy-preserving  
- Resilient to connectivity failures  
- Reduced cloud cost  
Vital for smart homes, wearables, and industrial IoT.

---

### **Q3. What constraints must AI models satisfy for edge deployment?**
**A3.**  
- Low memory usage (<10–100 MB)  
- Low compute cost (few GFLOPS)  
- Low energy consumption  
Techniques:  
- Quantization  
- Model pruning  
- Knowledge distillation  
- Hardware-aware architecture search  

---

### **Q4. How does real-time AI differ from traditional ML?**
**A4.**  
Real-time AI must guarantee:  
- Deterministic timing  
- Predictable latency  
- Bounded worst-case execution  
Used in medical CPS, drones, and smart manufacturing.  
Models must be small, interpretable, and latency-optimized.

---

### **Q5. What is federated learning, and how does it apply to IoT ecosystems?**
**A5.**  
Federated learning enables thousands of devices to train a shared model **without uploading raw data**.  
Applications:  
- Smart keyboards  
- Wearables  
- Smart meters  
Challenges:  
- Device heterogeneity  
- Communication limits  
- Privacy leakage in gradients  

---

### **Q6. What is TinyML, and what value does it bring?**
**A6.**  
TinyML brings ML inference to ultra-low-power microcontrollers (1–10 mW).  
Enables:  
- Wake-word detection  
- Anomaly detection in motors  
- Predictive maintenance  
- Wildlife monitoring  
TinyML models are highly optimized through quantization and architecture search.

---

### **Q7. How does anomaly detection work in cyber-physical systems?**
**A7.**  
Using time-series models (LSTM, transformers), statistical methods, or hybrid physical-neural models.  
Common anomalies: sensor faults, actuator failures, cyber attacks.  
Real-time anomaly detection is critical for industrial safety and reliability.

---

### **Q8. What is the role of digital twins in IoT and CPS?**
**A8.**  
Digital twins simulate physical systems for:  
- Predictive maintenance  
- Real-time monitoring  
- Optimization  
- Fault recovery  
Edge-based twins allow localized intelligence with cloud synchronization.

---

### **Q9. How does network latency affect CPS decision-making?**
**A9.**  
High latency can cause catastrophic failures (drone instability, industrial accidents).  
Solutions:  
- Edge inference  
- Local fallback control  
- Prioritized communication queues  
- Predictive buffering  

---

### **Q10. What is energy-aware AI inference?**
**A10.**  
AI models dynamically adapt precision, compute budget, and sampling rate to optimize battery life.  
Implemented using:  
- Dynamic neural networks (early exit)  
- Adaptive sampling  
- Lightweight architectures  

---

### **Q11. What attacks target AI-enabled CPS?**
**A11.**  
- Sensor spoofing (GPS, IMU)  
- Model poisoning  
- Adversarial examples  
- Replay attacks  
Security requires robust ML and end-to-end cryptographic integrity.

---

### **Q12. What is cooperative edge intelligence?**
**A12.**  
Edge devices collaborate by sharing partial inferences or compressed features rather than raw data.  
Improves bandwidth efficiency and robustness under network failures.

---

### **Q13. What is explainability in safety-critical CPS?**
**A13.**  
Explanations are required to ensure correctness and traceability of AI decisions (e.g., medical devices).  
Techniques:  
- Rule extraction  
- Counterfactual analysis  
- Saliency maps  
Explainability is mandatory for certification.

---

### **Q14. How does neuromorphic computing help edge AI?**
**A14.**  
Neuromorphic chips (Loihi, TrueNorth) use spiking neural networks that mimic brain efficiency.  
Benefits:  
- Ultra-low power  
- Event-driven computation  
- High parallelism  
Ideal for IoT sensors and mobile robots.

---

### **Q15. Why is CPS verification difficult?**
**A15.**  
Because CPS combines:  
- Continuous physical dynamics (differential equations)  
- Discrete software logic  
- Uncertain environments  
Formal verification becomes computationally expensive and often intractable at scale.

---

### **Q16. What are hybrid physical-ML models?**
**A16.**  
They combine physics-based equations with neural nets to:  
- Fill gaps where physical models are incomplete  
- Improve simulation fidelity  
- Provide better generalization  
Used in smart grids, HVAC systems, and industrial robotics.

---

### **Q17. What is self-healing IoT?**
**A17.**  
Systems that automatically detect failures and reconfigure themselves by:  
- Rolling back firmware  
- Re-routing network traffic  
- Switching to redundant modules  
Powered by anomaly detection + automated recovery logic.

---

### **Q18. How does CPS handle large-scale distributed coordination?**
**A18.**  
Using:  
- Distributed consensus  
- Time-sensitive networking (TSN)  
- Hierarchical control  
- Edge/cloud orchestration  
Essential for smart grids and industrial automation.

---

### **Q19. What are the biggest challenges in deploying AI on CPS at scale?**
**A19.**  
- Device heterogeneity  
- Strict latency budgets  
- Power constraints  
- Cybersecurity threats  
- Real-world calibration  
- Interoperability across vendors  

---

### **Q20. What future directions define edge + CPS AI research?**
**A20.**  
- Fully autonomous factories  
- Nano-edge AI (sub-1 mW learning)  
- AI-defined wireless networks  
- Joint edge-cloud training  
- Self-organizing IoT ecosystems  
These developments aim to unify physical, computational, and intelligent processes.
